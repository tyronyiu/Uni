Review Exercises Chapter 1

1.1 Main users of accounting information for a university would be e.g. the dean or divison head or any professor. Managerial accounting information could even be satisfaction of students. Students could also be users, having access to tuition fees and comparing to different unis. The number of students successfully graduated may be information necessary for governments giving tuition scholarships. Banks could get insights about the creditworthiness of that uni. Suppliers for e.g. chairs or tables asking themselves wether the uni could pay all the needed equipment they are ordering. 1.2 Management accounting is described as "The eyes and ears of management"

1.3 Accounting information helpful towards considering the launch of a new service:

  • Capital investment
  • Cost of product/service
  • Competitive analysis (what is in the market and what can we provide?)
  • Scale and of operations
  • Market demand
  • Pricing decision
  • Expected revenue
  • Worthfullness (ROI, Cost-benefit-analysis)
  • Market research
  • Cost of acquiring resources needed
  • Levels of competition
  • Supply chain management
